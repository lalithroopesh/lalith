Involvement @ USF

Founder and President, Bulls Science Olympiad

August 2022 - Present

Bulls Science Olympiad is a registered USF student organization whose mission is to further STEM education for high and middle school students. We aim to host a Science Olympiad tournament at USF and volunteer in-person at the regional competition at HCC and the state competition at USF. Our current project, a virtual invitational tournament open for teams across the country, is projected to serve around 800 students! We are looking for volunteers and team members, please follow the links before.

For more information on our organization, invitational, or to get involved visit our website at bullso.org.

Also follow @bulls.scioly on Instagram for updates!

Planning Committee Chair, Engineering Biology Core

September 2022 - Present

Engineering Biology Core is one of USF's premier student-led research organizations, hosting and funding our own projects alongside creating outreach events on campus. My position oversees social media and outreach events, along with playing a role in projects. I currently am a research lead on our MCPyV Host Interactions project and a strategist for our Neurotransmitter Modeling project.

For more information on our organization, visit @ebc.usf on Instagram!

Co-founder and Treasurer, Settle Out Club

September 2021 - Present

Settle Out USF is a club dedicated to fulfilling a huge USF need: get our diverse population used to Tampa and host fun events for people to get to know each other! We host a multitude of events at USF and take field trips to places around Tampa. As treasurer, I am responsible for budgeting, applying for funding, and working with Student Government and Student Business Services for every event. It was a great position to get used to USF bureaucracy that has set up my success in other endeavors.

For more information on our organization, visit @settleoutusf on Instagram!

Other Notable Positions

  • Honors Peer Mentor, Judy Genshaft Honors College (June 2022 - Present) - As part of this position, I teach a section of IDH 2930 Honors Foundations, which is a required class for all freshmen in the Honors College. Over the summer, I worked with other peer mentors to create curricula and I teach once a week during the fall. Huge thanks to my Peer Mentor Lead Connor Murray and our program's Director Allison Cousino for this opportunity!
  • Events Committee, Honors College Student Council (September 2022 - Present) - As a part of the events committee, I help brainstorm and plan events for honors students throughout the year. These can range from trivia nights to park picnics, anything that helps form bonds within the honors community.
  • Mayor, Juniper Hall Council (September 2021 - May 2022) - Similar to the Honors Council Student Council, this position involved planning events for the residents of Juniper Hall. We also collaborated with the hall councils of Poplar and Magnolia residence halls to put on large events! This lead to the formation of this year's JAMP Area council, which unfortunately doesn't have a schedule that works well with me.
